# Quality bar

Reject and regenerate if any fail:

- [ ] **White background**, generous empty space — not a busy or colored scene.
- [ ] **One idea only** — not a multi-panel explainer crammed into one frame
      (unless a mini-comic was intended).
- [ ] **The avatar is performing the action** and is clearly the subject —
      not standing in a corner as decoration.
- [ ] **The avatar is recognizable** and consistent with the reference
      (shape, colors, proportions).
- [ ] **Labels are short** (1–4 words) and few, spelled correctly, away from
      the avatar's face. No title; no structure name written on the image.
- [ ] **Reads at article width** — the metaphor is legible as a thumbnail.
- [ ] **Not** a photo, logo, corporate infographic, formal flowchart, or UI.

## Character integrity (the most common drift)

- [ ] **One silhouette, one accent.** The avatar reads as its own simple shape
      with exactly its one accent part — no extra accent colors, no new parts
      grown for this scene.
- [ ] **Limbs stay on-model.** A stubby arm stays stubby and joins the body
      cleanly — never stretched into a long bar / cable / lever across the scene,
      and never an extra floating or mid-body limb. A tool is *held* in a hand or
      sits in the scene, never sprouting from the torso. Re-roll if a limb
      morphed to reach something; restage so the reach is natural.
- [ ] **The avatar is opaque and in front.** No ground line, table edge, or prop
      passes *through* its body; background lines stop at its silhouette.

## Register check

- [ ] **Editorial** (default): one caught move, no diagram machinery — no rows of
      boxes, no flow spine. If it became a chart, it drifted to the wrong
      register; restage as a single move.
- [ ] **Explainer** (only when asked, or the idea IS a structure): 3–6 hand-drawn
      stations, one flow direction, the avatar working a key station — still no
      title block, legend, grid, or corner heading. See `references/composition.md`.

If the model misspells a long label, drop the label and regenerate. If the look
drifted, restate the style and regenerate rather than editing. Re-roll a drifted
character (restate the spec); don't try to paint over it.
